Help us test a new way to protect your data

You're invited to take part in a brief research study evaluating UCLDP, a user-centric privacy framework developed for a PhD dissertation at the University of Washington.

In plain terms: UCLDP lets you — not a company — decide how your data is protected. Your feedback helps us check if regular people find it useful and easy to use.

What you'll do (about 25 minutes)

  • Answer a few questions about yourself before starting.
  • Try an interactive demo. See how privacy settings affect your data.
  • Try a short "Option A or Option B" preference dialogue.
  • Complete a short survey with rating questions at the end.

What we record

  • Your answers in the demo and survey.
  • Any feedback you write.
  • A random ID created in your browser.
  • How long you spent on the study.

What we do NOT collect

  • Your name, email, or IP address.
  • Any personal data. All examples in the demo are fake.

You're in control

  • You can close this page any time.
  • If you close the page before submitting, no data will be recorded.
  • Your answers can't be traced back to you.
  • Anonymous response data will be retained for at least 5 years following publication, in line with university data retention policy.
  • Results may appear in academic papers.

    🎯 Step 2 — The interactive demo

    How this privacy tool works

    When apps collect your data, they often add a bit of random noise to hide your details. This is called privacy noise.

    The trade-off is simple:

    • More noise = better privacy, but the data becomes less useful.
    • Less noise = more useful data, but more risk of leaking personal info.
    The amount of noise is set by a number called ε (epsilon). Today, a company picks ε for you. Our tool lets you pick it yourself.

    Step 3 — Smart noise: not all data is equal

    The old way adds the same amount of noise to everything. Our way (called MIC-DP) gives less noise to the data that matters most and more noise to less important data. Same total privacy. Better results.

    Each home appliance has a different importance for predicting power use
    👉 Click any appliance below to see how the two methods treat it differently.
    🎛️ Try it: adjust how "smart" the noise is
    Move the slider to change how aggressively our method shifts noise toward less important data. Watch the green bars below rearrange.
    α = 1.5

    Old way — same noise for everything

    Every appliance gets the same amount of noise. The fridge (which barely matters) gets the same protection as the heating (which matters a lot). That wastes privacy budget on less useful info.

    Our way — smart noise

    Important data gets less noise (stays accurate). Less important data gets more noise (extra privacy). The total amount of privacy is the same — just spread more wisely.

    Bonus: works for groups too
